About



The latest K-pop sensation to take the world by storm, SOMI is an artist of both incredible talent and undeniable appeal. The Canadian-Dutch-Korean singer/songwriter recently signed to Interscope Records as part of its partnership with THEBLACKLABEL (an affiliate of YG Entertainment)—a turn of events that gives SOMI the rare distinction of being one of the few solo K-pop acts to land a record deal in the U.S. Proving her tremendous star power, the 19-year-old artist instantly delivered on that promise with the arrival of her Interscope debut “What You Waiting For,” a larger-than-life anthem whose video surpassed 7.5 million views on YouTube in under 24 hours. As her first new music since “Birthday,” “What You Waiting For” marks the start of an exciting new era for SOMI. Bigger, bolder, and more self-assured, the hypnotic and high-energy track finds SOMI sharing a writing credit with BLACKPINK writer/producer Teddy, and seamlessly shifting from Korean to English in her lyrics. Meanwhile, the visual for “What You Waiting For” offers an outrageous escape into a beautifully warped world all her own. With more new music on the way, SOMI is sure to continue her rise to the forefront of the global pop scene.

MILESTONES

BEING PART OF THE TOP FIVE OF FORBES KOREA’S POWER CELEBRITY YEARS SINCE 2014

EXO first appeared on Forbes Korea’s Power Celebrity list in 2014, securing the fifth spot on the list. The following year, the boys went on to top the list — a feat they replicated in 2016. Although they dropped to the fourth spot for the years 2017 and 2018, they are still the only boy group to have made the top five list for five consecutive years.

FIRST SOUTH KOREAN ARTIST IN 12 YEARS TO SELL OVER ONE MILLION COPIES OF AN ALBUM

XOXO was indeed a breakthrough album, not only for EXO, but for South Korea’s music industry as well. The first studio album of the boy group which was released in 2013 broke records as it becomes the first album in 12 years released by a South Korean artist to sell more than one million copies
