About



South Korean musician J-Hope is best known as a member of the K-pop boy band . He made his official solo debut in 2018 with Hope World. The next year, he scored a hit with his single "Chicken Noodle Soup" with Becky G.
Born Jung Ho-seok in Gwangju, he developed a love of dance at a young age, later delving into singing and rapping. He made his debut in in 2013. The band went on to become one of the biggest K-pop acts of a generation, breaking into the U.S. market with their hip-hop-influenced dance sound. In 2018, Jung became the third member to embark on a solo career with his debut mixtape, Hope World. In addition to becoming just the fifth Korean artist to chart on the Billboard 200 (following BoA, Taeyang, JongHyun, and G-Dragon), he also became the highest-charting solo K-pop act on the Billboard 200 to date. Landing at number 63 on the main chart, Hope World also topped the World Albums chart. That same year, he hit number one on the Billboard 200 twice with BTS for Love Yourself: Tear and Love Yourself: Answer.
In 2019, a duet with Becky G, "Chicken Noodle Soup," shot up the charts across the globe. Popularized with a social media dance challenge, the track topped the Billboard World Digital Songs chart and even edged into the Hot 100. ~ Neil Z. Yeung, Rovi

MILESTONES

Hope World peaked at No. 38 on the Billboard 200

In March 2018, J-Hope made his solo debut in with his first mixtape, Hope World. The album debuted at No. 63 on the Billboard 200 and peaked at No. 38. At the time, Hope World made J-Hope the highest-charting solo Korean artist on the chart. Over the past year, J-Hope solo work has received many accolades and broken records.

J-Hope dethroned BIGBANG‘s G-Dragon as Spotify’s #1 most-followed Korean solo artist.

Back in April, J-Hope dethroned BIGBANG‘s G-Dragon as Spotify’s #1 most-followed Korean solo artist. At the time, he had 1,341,112 followers (one more follower than G-Dragon had).

J-Hope is the first Korean solo artist to reach 2 million followers on Spotify

J-Hope is the first Korean solo artist to reach 2 million followers on the platform. Even more impressive is the fact that J-Hope reached this milestone with only seven songs, all from Hope World!
